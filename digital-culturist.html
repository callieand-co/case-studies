The Product

An AI tool for the people who see patterns before anyone else.

Digital Culturist, created by strategist Carissa Estreller, solves a problem every cultural researcher knows intimately: the scattered mess of screenshots, bookmarks, saved TikToks, and half-formed observations that never get organized into actual insight. The platform lets users upload cultural fragments — screenshots, quotes, articles, memes — alongside personal notes about why each piece matters, then uses AI to identify thematic clusters and surface patterns.

The Approach

Community-first GTM from concept through beta.

  • Led full go-to-market strategy from concept through MVP, defining positioning, audience segmentation, and channel strategy for a product that didn't fit existing categories
  • Built beta acquisition strategy using organic community channels only — Substack, private Slack, and word-of-mouth within strategist and cultural research networks
  • Designed the beta community program with monthly virtual meetups, product previews, direct feedback sessions, and early testing access
  • Ran product reveal events to generate momentum and convert curiosity into committed beta signups
  • Closed user feedback loops so Carissa could iterate the product based on real practitioner needs, not assumptions

The Results

150+ beta members. 7 days. Zero dollars.

  • Grew beta community to 150+ members in 7 days through organic channels only
  • Established repeatable community-led acquisition framework for ongoing growth
  • Built CRM and email infrastructure that scales with user base from day one
  • Created a beta community with active Slack engagement, monthly meetups, and direct product influence
  • Product on track for Q1 2026 launch with a built-in audience of founding members

What I Did

Fractional CMO — full-stack GTM.

  • Brand positioning and messaging
  • Beta community design and management
  • CRM architecture (HubSpot) and email onboarding flows
